Virat Kohli: The Modern Maestro

A journey through the life and unparalleled achievements of one of cricket's greatest.

Explore His Legacy

Early Life

Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, Virat Kohli's journey into cricket began early. From the vibrant streets of Uttam Nagar, his passion for the game was clear even as a three-year-old, when he would swing a bat and challenge his father to bowl.

His father, Prem Kohli, a criminal lawyer, saw his son's talent and enrolled him in the West Delhi Cricket Academy in 1998. Under coach Rajkumar Sharma, Virat honed his skills with relentless dedication, laying the foundation for his future success.

A defining moment came on December 18, 2006, when his father passed away from a stroke. Despite this loss, an 18-year-old Virat played a Ranji Trophy match for Delhi the next day, scoring 90 runs, showcasing his resilience and commitment.

Progressing through age-group cricket, he captained the Delhi U-17 team and later transferred to Saviour Convent School in ninth grade to focus on cricket, a decision that set the stage for his rise to global stardom.

Career Highlights

U19 World Cup Victory (2008)

Virat led India to the U19 World Cup title in Malaysia, scoring 235 runs in 6 matches. His leadership and batting prowess marked him as a future star.

ODI Debut & Rise (2008-2011)

Debuting on August 18, 2008, against Sri Lanka, Virat quickly made his mark with a century in 2009. He played a key role in the 2011 ODI World Cup victory, scoring a century against Bangladesh.

Test Debut & Captaincy (2011-2022)

Kohli debuted in Tests on June 20, 2011, against West Indies. As India’s most successful Test captain, he led to 40 wins in 68 matches, securing historic overseas victories.

T20 World Cup Champion (2024)

A pivotal member of the 2024 T20 World Cup-winning team, Virat earned Player of the Match in the final with a match-defining 76 off 59 balls.

The Chase Master

Known as "The Chase Master", Virat excels in run-chases, combining calculated aggression with flawless innings-building in limited-overs cricket.

Unmatched Consistency

Virat’s consistency across formats is unmatched, with high averages and regular centuries. He is the only batter to achieve 900 rating points in all three formats.

Records & Awards

Key Batting Records

  • Most ODI Centuries: Surpassed Sachin Tendulkar with 51+ centuries in ODIs.
  • Most Runs in T20Is: Leads with over 4,188 runs in Men’s T20Is.
  • Fastest to ODI Milestones: Reached 8,000 (175 innings), 9,000 (194 innings), 10,000 (205 innings), 11,000 (222 innings), 12,000 (242 innings), and 13,000 (267 innings) ODI runs.
  • Most Successful Indian Test Captain: Secured 40 Test victories in 68 matches.
  • First to Win All Four ICC Titles: Won U19 World Cup (2008), ODI World Cup (2011), Champions Trophy (2013), and T20 World Cup (2024).
  • Most Player of the Series Awards: Holds 21 awards (11 ODI, 7 T20, 3 Test).
  • Most Runs in a Single ODI World Cup: Scored 765 runs in 2023.
  • Most ODI Centuries while Chasing: Notched 27 centuries in successful chases.
  • Highest IPL Run-scorer: Leads with over 7,263 runs.
  • 300 ODIs, 100 Tests, 100 T20Is: Only player to achieve this milestone across formats.

Prestigious Awards

  • Arjuna Award: Received in 2013 for outstanding sports achievements.
  • Padma Shri: Honored in 2017, India’s fourth-highest civilian award.
  • Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna: India’s highest sporting honor, awarded in 2018.
  • ICC Awards Sweep (2018): First to win ICC ODI Player, Test Player, and Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy in one year.
  • Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy: Won in 2017, 2018, 2020, and named ICC Cricketer of the Decade (2011-2020).
  • ICC ODI Player of the Year: Awarded in 2012, 2017, 2018, 2023.
  • ICC Test Player of the Year: Honored in 2018.
  • ICC Spirit of Cricket Award: Received in 2019.
  • Wisden Leading Cricketer: Recognized in 2017, 2018, 2019.
